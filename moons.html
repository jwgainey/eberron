The Moons of Eberron

Similarly to baseline DnD, existance is made up of intersecting metaphysical planes. Unlike baseline, They do not cleanly line up with the four elements or alignments, or even dieties. There are twelve moons believed to be physical projections of the planes that make up the universe with one possible additional plane related to dreaming. In general, when using names, the moon and plane can be used interchangably. It is currently unknown, aside from simple observation, what the moons are actually like, with only very little documentaion of interplanar travel found left behind by the Giants. Everything known from modern sources come from observations on their orbits around Eberron, or their effects on Manefest Zones throughout the world.

Manefest Zones are areas ranging between the size of a small village up to possibly the size of an entire continent that are heavily affected by a particular moon. In general, their effects can ebb and flow with time, but some appear to be permanent, or at the very least exhibit a low, constant effect. Somewhat related, the moons' position in orbit can have effects similar to a manefest zone on a greater scale. Scientific study of these areas have answered many questions about the nature of magic in the universe as well as expose some unsettling truths.

The lunar cycles can also have a direct impact on the way some living beings are born. Some of the moons are responsible for the planetouched races such as Genasi, Tieflings, and Aasimar, wich tends to only affect that particular child, although sensitvity to particular moons seem to run in lineages. Although there are constelations in the sky, it is the moons are used as a sort of zodiac system or for divination. In fact, very little is known about the stars due to the overwhelming presence of twelve moons in comparison. Some myths state that each of the Proginator Wyrms created these moons before joining to become the planet Eberron.


Siberys Ring

Not actually a moon, but it is the closest heavenly body to Eberron. Named after and in myth believed to be the shattered remains of the Proginator Siberys. The ring is made from Siberys dragonshards, and so is believed by some to be the reason magic is even possible within the world to begin with.

Irian

  • Positive Energy
  • Life/Light
  • Siberys

This moon functions as the setting's sun. By comparison it is much larger compared to the other moons, possibly around the size of Eberron itself. It is the largest source of light for the world, but not heat. Very little is known about the moon itself even from Giant research. Its manefest zones are found most often in Aundaire, The Eldeen Reaches and the Talentia Plains. Creatures born during their heights are often perfectly healthy and tend to be stronger than others of similar age, but that tends to even out later in life. The ground is extremely fertile, and crops seem to grow much faster, sometimes allowing multiple harvests in a single season. Healing and restoration magic is also much stronger here, while necromancy tends to be less effective.

Fernia

  • Fire
  • Order/Summer
  • Kyber/Siberys

This moon is the second brightest in the sky. Most heat in the world is geothermal, but Fernia seems to also regulate it somewhat, with summer cycles being closely related to its orbit. Manefest zones typically result in deserts in hte middle of otherwise fertile fields. Some zones also have the strange effect of preventing creatures from lying, or dispelling illusions. From Giant documentation on the moon, it is belived that certain tribes of Devils and Angels living there are responsible for this more esoteric effect. Fire magic and magic relating to the truth or despelling other magic works better, while magic related to deception tends to fail more frequently.

Syrania

  • Air
  • Peace/Gravity
  • Siberys/Eberron

According to Giant texts, some travel took place to this plane ages ago. It is described as endless fields with constantly perfect weather, never reaching night, only twilight at its darkest. It is here that the trees that the floatwood used in airship construction is harvested from seems to originate. Manefest zones vary a bit compared to others, with one in Arenal being the only place in Eberron that allows floatwood growth, and the one where Sharn is located, altering the gravity, enabling it be to be built as high as it is. Some smaller zones also house idyllic villages that seem to have avoided effects of the Last War. Wind and air magic, alongside magic that effects gravity sees more potential and creatures have an easier time resisting magic that forces them to act against their will.

Risia

  • Ice/Lightning
  • Winter/Storms
  • Kyber/Eberron

Another plane that the Giants seem to have made some contact with. The land is mostly frozen and besieged in constantly raging storms. It is believed that the entire continent of Everice is a manefest zone of Risia and Stormhome is the result of a much smaller, weaker one. Watching its position in the sky in relation to Fernia and Syrania allows experts to fairly accuratly predict the weather and how long or harsh a coming winter will be. Due to most manefest zones taking place over the ocean or on a inhospitibal continent, it is not known what magic, other than cold, electric, and wind are effected.

Thelanis

  • Fey
  • Seasons/Emotions
  • Siberys/Eberron

Manefest zones of this moon tend to occure far from civilization, with druid sects only coming across them rarely. Large, sprawling forests grow around thise zones as well, making the directly affected zone even more difficult to locate. It is however known, that these forests often act under the influence of their own weather and seasonal patterns, with each season sometimes lasting years on end. Some druids have also reported hightened emotions and senses when nearing some of the smaller or temporary zones. Some myths tell of strange creatures native to the plane who are the ones actually controlling the climate, but are notoriously fickle to deal with.

Lamannia

  • Lycanthropy
  • Strenght/The Wild
  • Eberron/Kyber

Belived to be the source of lycanthropy, due to some Fey influences and the fact that it is in its full state far more often than the other moons are. (Barring Irian, wich is essentially always full.) These zones are rather hard to study, due to seemingly only appearing far from civilization, with even the druids having difficulty finding them. It is believed that Lamannia gives barbarians some of their supernatural ability alongside the moon Shavarath. Like Thelanis study is primarily drawn from surrounding wilderness rather than directly. It is infered from these studies that Lammania seems to make magic that changes the body or senses work better and longer, and makes mind affecting magic struggle to take hold.

Daanvi

  • Knowlege
  • Magic/Innovation
  • Eberron/Kyber

This is one of the few planes with very little information from the Giants, due mostly to its very long orbit, nearly 1000 years! It is thought that each cycle has brought a new age of technology or a rise between different species. Of course, civilizatin still improves and changes even without Daanvi's presence, but it also cannot be ignored that the greatest jumps are when it is near. It is also said that the dragonmarks first started appearing during one of Daanvi's zeniths. Its manefest zones are rather subtle compared to others, but in particular, ritual or enchanting magic seems to gain the most effects. It is also theorized that a few 'null zones', where magic is largely unable to function, are a type of manefest zone of Daanvi.

Kythri

  • Transmutation/Dimensional
  • Change/Liquid
  • Eberron

This strange moon seems to have no orbit at all, either wildly flailing across the sky or sometimes appearing in a difference section of space from night to night. It is because of this that it is believed that all magic involving transporting matter is energized by this particualr moon. Manefest zones are also unstable, but in a much different way. Inristingly, through clever tracking procedures and some luck, researchers discovered that this particular moon is completly liquid, although the exact material is still unknown. The ground in these zone are plyable by the sheer will of individuals within. This effect may have lead to the beginnings of transmutation magic. Thankfully, most of these zones are temporary and as of yet, has not happened in any major population area. Transmutaion and conjoration magic tend both act quicker and last longer under the effects of this moon. Dispellation and other abjuration effects aren't quite as useful as normal. If a manefest zone appears near or even within another pre-existing zone, magic fluctuates wildly and is rather unpridictable.

Shavarath

  • War
  • Violence/Martial Prowess
  • Ebberon

This plane is commonly associated acts of war and violence. Manefest zones only show up in battlfields, in some cases, reports of Fiends fighting Angels followed great battles, but with no physical evidence to support this. It is a common superstition that weapons forged on battlegrounds are much deadlier, but come with a curse that the wielde must constantly enact violence or thir soul will be consumed by the weapon. While some weapons have been confirmed to be of better quality, it is difficult to coraborate if they were made under the influence of the moon. Alongside Lamannia, it is also belived that a Barbarian gets their inhuman physical ability from this plane. In general, weapons or otherwise physical attacking does greater damage, while non-damaging spells have a harder time taking hold.

Dolurrh

  • Negative Energy
  • Death/Salt
  • Khyber

The land of the dead. Other than the very pious, it seems that all souls go here after expiring. Due to the rarity of people retaining their 'memory' during their death after being revived, there is not much information on the afterlife, but descriptions believed to be related to this moon are relatively common. Souls of the recently deceased are seen aimlessly wandering around while strange dark shapes seem to stalk many of them. The world resembles Eberron from descriptions, but in ruins and under an unatural darkness. The only unique structure resebles a large castle or fortress in the distance. It is theorized that early contact with this plane lead to several religions with a more positive afterlife being developed. Those belonging to these religions that have been revived either have no memory, or a near textbook description of that religion's version of of the afterlife. Manefest zones tend to weaken healing magic, and those who died in its borders will always arise by the next day as some sort of undead. The zones tend not to be able to support life, with water becoming too salty, regardless of it location, and land dries out, becoming small salt plains. Karnnath is home to some of the few permanent zones, wich is what enabled them to amass one of the most frightful armies in world history.

Mabar

  • Darkness
  • Night/Disapearance
  • Kyber

Tracking of this moon has proven difficult due to it always being in its new state. Sometimes, under the right alignment of moons and stars, you can see a blank space moving across the sky, this is believed to be Mabar. Equally hard to keep track of is its manafest zones. All that is known about them is that they occure in extreme darkness and cause objects and sometimes people to disapear. It is currently unknown where they go. This is rarer than it sounds as most sources of light, including moonlight or even candlelight dispells this effect. It greatly affects magic that changes sight and illusions, and hinders dispelling or countering magic.

Xoriat

  • Psychic Energy
  • Fear/Chaos
  • ???

This moon has no strong association with any of the Proginator Dragons, and is unkown how or even when it was formed. Characterized by a strange, green glow, this moon often causes uneasyness to people who look at it for too long. It is believed to be the original home of the Daelkyr, strange, powerful beings who attacked Eberron millenia ago. It was during the war with the Daelkyr that a group of druids known as the Gatekeepers created numerous great sigils that sealed off Xoriat from the world. The moon still orbits, but whatever energies it is host to is inaccesable to the world and any manafest zones were 'locked' in place. These zones tend to generate a sense of unease and are remarkably easy to get lost in. Magic that affects fear and clouds judgement are more potent, as well as reducing resistances to such effects. Psychic effects and other forms of mental magic can also have unpredictable side effects.

Dal Quor

  • Psychic Energy
  • Dreams
  • ???

A ring of astroids looping around much of the planet's system. Believed to once have been a moon, but destroyed by the Giants in an attempt to stop an invasion of nightmare creatures. This is difficult to prove or disprove due to the Giant's disapearance from the worl shortly after this war. It is believed by some that the plane associated is a shared dreamscape amongst mortals. Some evidence has arisen for this, mostly regarding proof of the aforemention nightmares, and through corroberation between those with psionic abilities. There are no known manefest zones and other than a link to psionc energy, does not seem to affect magic.
