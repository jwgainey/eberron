The Metascape of Eberron


Like most settings, existence is made up of intersecting metaphysical planes. As written, they are pretty much just renamed planes from the other settings. So Ive made so that they do not cleanly line up with the four elements or alignments, or even deities. This ranges between a plane aligning with multiple elements or metaphysical concepts. There are twelve moons believed to be physical projections of the planes that make up the universe with one additional plane related to dreaming. In general, when using names, the moon and plane can be used interchangeably, they technically have different names, but I'm not keeping up with that. In addition, I rule that the 'plane' is simply the energy, and the moon itself is the physical embodiment. So say you cast a fire spell, the energy comes from the plane; the energy field, while if you plane shift to Fernia, it will be a physical place HEAVILY influenced by fire instead of a physical pocket within the concept of fire.


Most information about the moons come from accounts of the Giants, who were able to move across them at level not seen today. Much of this information is seen as highly fantastical, being that researchers are only able to observe their elemental associations by their orbit. The orbits have a great impact on Manifest Zones across all of Eberron. These zones are areas ranging between the size of a small village up to the size of an entire continent that are heavily affected by a particular moon. In general, their effects can ebb and flow with time, but some appear to be permanent, or at the very least exhibit a low, constant effect. Somewhat related, the moons' position in orbit and its lunar cycle can have effects similar to a manifest zone on a greater scale. Scientific study of these areas have answered many questions about the nature of magic in the universe as well as expose some unsettling truths.


The lunar cycles can also have a direct impact on the way some living beings are born. Some of the moons are responsible for the planetouched races such as Genasi, Tieflings, and Aasimar. This tends to only affect that particular child, although sensitivity to particular moons seem to run in lineages. Although there are constellation in the sky, it is the moons that are used as a sort of zodiac system or for divination. In fact, very little is known about the stars due to the overwhelming presence of twelve moons in comparison. Some myths state that each of the Progenitor Wyrms created these moons before joining to become the planet Eberron. Also included are aspects of the world itself.


The Moons and Other Bodies


Irian

Positive Energy

Light

Life

Irian: the Eternal Dawn

This moon functions as the setting's sun. By comparison it is much larger compared to the other moons, close to the size of Eberron itself. It is the largest source of light for the world, but not its heat. Its manifest zones cause the ground to be extremely fertile, sometimes allowing multiple harvests a year. It is believed by some to have associations with the Couatl of legend. Healing and restoration magic is also much stronger here, while necromancy tends to be less effective.

Fernia

Fire

Law

Heat

Fernia: the Sea of Fire

This moon is the second brightest in the sky, functioning as a sort of second sun. Most heat in the world is geothermal, but is highly regulated by Fernia, likewise, summer cycles are closely related to its orbit. Manifest zones typically result in deserts in the middle of otherwise fertile lands. Some zones also have the strange effect of preventing creatures from lying, or disabling illusions. Documentation from Giant contact relates this esoteric effect to the Devils and Angels that make their home here. Fire magic and magic relating to the truth or dispelling other magic works better, while magic related to the cold or deceptions tends to fail more frequently.

Syrania

Air

Gravity

Peace

Syrania: the Azure Sky

According to Giant texts, much travel took place to this plane ages ago. It is described as endless fields with consistently perfect weather, never reaching night, only twilight at its darkest. Manifest Zones are fairly rare with the only documented areas being Sharn and a forest in Arenal. Wind and air magic, alongside magic that effects gravity sees more potential and creatures have an easier time resisting magic that forces them to act against their will.

Thelanis

Fae

Seasons

Stories

Thalanis: the Faerie Court

Home to the Fae, the related zones are rarely interacted with anyone other than druidic sects, who typically keep these locations secret. It can be infered that this is a source of their powers. The Archfey have near total control over the plane, and through this, the manifest zones as well. There are many stories of pockets of wood having a different season than the surrounding area, and some even having a completely different flow in time. Effects that charm or dazzle have a much stronger sway under Thalanis' influence as well as making magic in general more difficult to dispel or counter.

Lamannia

Wilds

Lycanthropy

Primal Energy

Lamannia: the Twilight Forest

It is widely believed that the lycanthropic curse originated from this wild plane of existence. Also home to some of the wilder, darker Fae, Lamannia houses all manner of beasts, some long thought to be extinct. Primal elementals are also commonly summoned from this realm, as they have a slightly weaker connection to it compared to one of their own element. It is also believed to be a source of power for the inhuman rage of barbarians. Conjuration magic is much easier to perform under its influence as well as enhancing certain magical non-spell abilities. Magic that has little to with the primal forces of nature or destruction find themselves easier to be resisted however.

Risia

Ice

Lightning

Fortitude

Risia: the Freezing Tempest

Another plane that the Giants seem to have made much contact with. The land is mostly frozen and besieged in constantly raging storms. It is believed that the entire continent of Everice is a manifest zone of Risia. Watching its position in the sky in relation to Fernia and Syrania allows experts to fairly accurately predict the weather and how long or harsh a coming winter will be. Due to most manifest zones taking place over the ocean or on a inhospitable continent, it is not known what is affected other than the associated elemental eneries.

Shavarath

War

Violence

Destruction

Shavarath: the Battleground

This plane is commonly associated with acts of war and violence. Manifest zones only develop in large-scale battlefields with combating Fiends and Angels sometimes being spotting in these areas. It is a common superstition that weapons forged on battlegrounds are much deadlier, but they can become cursed or even sentient weapons. Alongside Lamannia, it is also believed that barbarians get their superhuman physical abilities from this plane. In general, weapons or otherwise physical attacking does greater damage, while non-damaging spells have a harder time taking hold.

Daanvi

Order

Magic

Innovation/Stagnation

Daanvi: the Perfect Order

This is one of the few planes with very little information, due mostly to its very long orbit, nearly 1000 years! It is thought that each cycle has brought a new age of technology or a rise between different species. Of course, civilization still improves and changes even without Daanvi's presence, but it also cannot be ignored that the greatest jumps are when it is near. It is also said that the dragonmarksfirst started appearing during one of Daanvi's zeniths. Its manifest zones are rather subtle compared to others, but in particular, ritual or enchanting magic seems to gain the most effects. It is also theorized that 'null zones', where magic is largely unable to function,are a type of manifest zone of Daanvi.

Kythri

Chaos

Magic

Fluidity

Kythri: the Churning Chaos

This strange, apparently liquid, moon seems to have no orbit at all, either wildly flailing across the sky or sometimes appearing in a difference section of space from night to night. It is because of this that it is believed that all magic involving transporting matter is energized by this particular moon. Transmutation and conjuration magic tend both are stronger and last longer under the effects of this moon. Dispelling and other abjuration effects aren't quite as useful as normal. If a manifest zone appears near or even within another pre-existing zone, magic fluctuates wildly and is rather unpredictable. It should be noted that to date, no Kythri zone has interacted with a Daanvi zone.

Dolurrh

Positve/Negative Energy

Death

Salt

Dolurrh: the Realm of the Dead

The land of the dead. Other than the very pious, it seems that all souls go here after expiring. Shades of mortals wander the empty fields and valleys until they fade away, believed by some to be reincarnated soon after. Due to its unique mixture of positive and negative energy, true resurrection is channeled from this plane. Undead created from this plane tend to be the harmless or mindless sort, such as a standard, directionless zombie or a ghost held back by regrets. It is also home to highly territorial spirits that attack living interlopers. It is unknown what happens to the living who die there, so it is typically not recommended to attempt to visit. Unoffensive necromantic magic works much better in these zones, although healing and turning undead is weakened somewhat.

Mabar

Negative Energy

The Void

Darkness

Mabar: the Endless Night

Tracking of this moon has proven difficult due to it always being in a darkened new state. Equally hard to keep track of is its manifest zones. All that is known about them is that they occur in extreme darkness and cause objects and sometimes people to disappear. This is rarer than it sounds, as most sources of light, including moonlight or even candlelight dispels this effect. Like Dolurrh, Mabar can cause undead phenomenon, but it always results in hostile, ravenous undead. Offensive necromancy and magic involving trickery work much better under its influence, but can also have unintended drawbacks or backlashes on the user.

Xoriat

Psychic Energy

Life

Chaos

Xoriat: the Living Madness

Characterized by a strange, green glow, this moon often causes uneasiness to people who look at it for too long. It is the original home of the Daelkyr, strange, powerful beings who attacked Eberron millennia ago. It was during the war with the Daelkyr that a group of druids known as the Gatekeepers created numerous great sigils that sealed off Xoriat from the world. The moon still orbits, but whatever energies it is host to is inaccessible to the world and any manifest zones were 'locked' in place. These zones tend to generate a sense of unease and are remarkably easy to get lost in. Magic that affects fear and clouds judgement are more potent, as well as reducing resistancesto such effects. Psychic abilities and other forms of mental magic can also have unpredictable side effects.

Dal Quor

Psychic Energy

Dreams

Isolation

Dal Quor: the World of Dreams

A ring of asteroids that loops around much of the planet's system. It is believed to have once been a moon, but was destroyed by the Giants in an attempt to stop an invasion of nightmare creatures. This is difficult to prove or disprove due to the Giant's disappearance from the world shortly after this war. It is believed by some that the plane associated is a shared dreamscape amongst mortals. Some evidence has arisen for this, mostly regarding existence of the aforementioned nightmares, and through corroboration between those with psionic abilities. There are no known manifest zones and other than a link to psionic energy.

Syberis Ring

Magic

Good

Light

Syberis Ring: the Dragon Above

The ring of dragonshards surrounding Eberron is named after the Proginator Wyrm due to legend that it is actually the remains of his shattered body after his betrayal by Kyber. It is commonly believed, although difficult to prove or disprove, that the ring is the source of magic in the world. As of yet, meteors from the ring are the only way to obtain the rare shards, with only two attempts to reach that far have taken place.

Eberron

Earth

Balance

Space

Eberron: the Dragon Between

The world itself. As of now, over 70% of the world has been explored and document, not including the precarious depths underground. Out of all the planetary bodies, Eberron seems to have a more or less equal balance of planar energies, especially when compared to the moons. The conflicting aspects could be a reason behind Eberron's tumultuous history, but also enables is limitless potential.

Kyber

Space

Evil

Isolation

Kyber: the Dragon Below

Avenging her brother, Eberron coiled around the treacherous Wyrm and became the planet encasing the strange landscapes of Kyber. The vast systems of caverns and tunnels are noted for making little physical sense. What may be a several day trip over land could take mere hours through the right tunnel. Collosal caves can be found stretching beyond where the ground should actually be, and tunneling from above finds no purchace. In most cases, this region is left alone, also due to the belief that the demonic spawn of Kyber make their home here.
